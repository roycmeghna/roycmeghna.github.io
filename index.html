Interpretable hierarchical Machine Learning for Mental Health

Understanding and predicting mental health is incredibly complex. Our daily digital footprints create vast amounts of behavioral data, but it's noisy, high-dimensional, and varies wildly between people, making standard models struggle.

Our Solution: Interpretable AI with I-HOPE

We developed I-HOPE, featuring a novel hierarchical model that translates complex raw behavioral data into understandable interaction patterns. By applying smart feature reduction, we simplified the data without losing vital predictive power, creating personalized and interpretable insights.

Impact & Results: ‚ú®

  • Achieved 91% prediction accuracy for student mental health outcomes ‚Äì a huge leap from the 60% baseline!
  • Successfully bridged the gap between raw user behavior and meaningful mental health indicators.
  • Enabled personalized predictions through interpretable modeling.

Tech Highlights: Python (TensorFlow, Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn, SHAP)

Distributed Neural Networks for IoB

Wearables are getting smarter, running complex AI tasks. But this drains power, leading to frustratingly short battery life. How can we run powerful AI without constant charging?

Our Solution: DistNN - Sharing the AI Load!

We created DistNN, a game-changing framework that splits neural network tasks between the tiny wearable device and a more powerful nearby hub (like your phone). We found the *optimal* split point to drastically cut the wearable's energy use and designed custom low-precision hardware for ultra-efficiency.

Impact & Results: üöÄ

  • Achieved ~1000x higher energy efficiency compared to running the AI on a GPU!
  • Consumed 11x less power on average than recent state-of-the-art AI chips for wearables.
  • Maintained real-time performance (30 fps) and high accuracy (0.9 SSIM for image reconstruction).
  • Redefines possibilities for powerful, practical AI on future wearable tech.

Tech Highlights: Distributed Neural Networks, Custom Figure of Merit (FoM), Low-Precision Fixed-Point Arithmetic, CNNs, Autoencoders.

Self-Supervised Learning with Squeeze and Excitation Networks for EEG

Analyzing brain signals (EEG) is crucial for Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCIs) and diagnosing neurological conditions. But real-world EEG is messy, often lacks labels, and processing it efficiently on low-power devices is tough.

Our Solution: SSL-SE-EEG - Smarter EEG Analysis, Less Data Needed!

We developed SSL-SE-EEG, a novel framework that combines clever Self-Supervised Learning (SSL) to learn from unlabeled EEG data with efficient Squeeze-and-Excitation Networks (SE-Nets). We also transform messy EEG signals into structured 2D images, making them easier for deep learning models to understand.

Impact & Results: üß†üí°

  • Achieved state-of-the-art accuracy on multiple benchmark EEG datasets (e.g., 91% on MindBigData, 85% on TUH-AB seizure).
  • Significantly reduces the need for expensive labeled data through SSL.
  • Enhanced robustness against noise artifacts common in real-world EEG.
  • Maintained minimal power overhead, making it ideal for real-time, low-power BCI applications.

Tech Highlights: Self-Supervised Learning (SSL), Squeeze-and-Excitation Networks (SE-Nets), EEG Signal Processing (as Images), Python (PyTorch/TensorFlow), MNE-Python.

Low Power Wearable ECG via Human Body Communication

Continuous ECG monitoring is vital, but traditional wearables can be bulky and require frequent charging. How can we make it truly seamless and low-power?

Our Solution: FlexECG - Powering ECG Through Your Body!

We created FlexECG, a flexible patch that harvests energy from body heat and uses the human body itself as a communication channel (HBC) to transmit ECG data. This radically reduces power needs compared to traditional wireless methods.

Impact & Results:‚ö°

  • Developed efficient HBC transmission/decoding code for ECG signals.
  • Achieved incredibly low power consumption (down to 20¬µW!).
  • Demonstrated robustness through experiments with multiple human subjects.
  • System power consumption is 4x lower than comparable state-of-the-art solutions.

Tech Highlights: PSoC Microcontroller, Human Body Communication (HBC), Energy Harvesting (TEGs), Analog Front-End Design, MATLAB, Python, PSoC Creator.

Electric Field-Based Intrathecal Drug Delivery (IDD)

Intrathecal Drug Delivery (IDD) offers long-term chronic pain relief by administering drugs directly to the central nervous system. However, current methods often struggle to guarantee precise delivery into the target Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) layer or involve high costs.

Our Solution: Sensing Location with Electric Fields

This project introduces a pioneering electronic IDD system based on electric fields. We leverage the unique electrical properties of CSF ‚Äì specifically, its higher conductivity compared to surrounding tissues at certain frequencies (tens of MHz). Our system uses a galvanic transmitter and a capacitive receiver operating at Electroquasistatic (EQS) frequencies to sense the environment.

Validation & Results: ‚ö°Ô∏è

  • Simulations using Ansys HFSS on the realistic "Neva" human body model were conducted.
  • Confirmed a difference (significant signal change) in electric field strength when the transmitter is correctly positioned within the CSF compared to adjacent layers (like fat or muscle).
  • This demonstrates the potential to accurately verify catheter placement for precise drug delivery.
  • To our knowledge, this is the first electric field-based approach proposed for enhancing IDD accuracy.

Tech Highlights: Ansys HFSS, Electromagnetics Simulation, Finite Element Method (FEM), Neva Body Model, Electroquasistatic (EQS) Fields, Bioimpedance/Conductivity Sensing.

AI for Safer Surgery: Predicting Anesthesia Depth

During surgery, precisely monitoring how deeply a patient is anesthetized is critical for their safety and comfort. Traditional methods can have limitations. Could AI offer a smarter, non-invasive way using readily available signals?

Our Solution: Listening to the Heart with Deep Learning

We explored using common physiological signals, like the heart's electrical activity (ECG) and pulse waves (PPG), recorded continuously during real surgeries. By applying advanced Deep Learning models (CNNs and LSTMs), we aimed to create an accurate, real-time indicator of the patient's anesthetic depth.

Impact & Results: ü©∫ü§ñ

  • Successfully processed complex ECG/PPG signals from surgical patients using MATLAB.
  • Developed and trained various deep learning architectures in Python (TensorFlow/Keras).
  • Achieved approximately 82% accuracy* in predicting clinical anesthesia depth using just ECG features.
  • Validated and shared the methodology through publication in the Biomedical Signal Processing and Control journal.
  • Demonstrates potential for AI to assist anesthesiologists with a data-driven, non-invasive tool.

Tech Highlights: Deep Learning (CNN, LSTM), Python (TensorFlow/Keras), Signal Processing, MATLAB, ECG/PPG Analysis.
